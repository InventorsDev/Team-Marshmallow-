Eligibility Criteria For Blood Donation

In order to ensure the safety and wellbeing of both donors and recipients, here is a detailed outline showing the requirements for individuals who wish to donate blood. Please read the following guidelines carefully to determine your eligibility for blood donation.

Age:

Donors must be between the ages of 17 and 65.
Minors may be eligible to donate with parental consent.

Weight:

Donors must weigh at least 45kg. This helps to ensure that the donor has sufficient blood volume to donate without experiencing adverse effects.

Health Conditions:

Individuals with certain medical conditions or health factors may be temporarily or permanently deferred from donating blood. These conditions may include but are not limited to:

  • History of heart disease, stroke or cardiovascular conditions
  • Active infectious diseases or febrile illness
  • Cancer or recent cancer treatment
  • Chronic kidney or liver disease
  • Blood disorders such as hemophilia or sickle cell disease

Medications:

Some medications may affect blood donation eligibility. Donors taking certain medications such as anticoagulants(blood thinners) or immunosuppressants(used to suppress the immune system) may be deferred.
To ensure the safety of both the donor and the recipient of the donated blood, it is important to provide accurate information bout any medications being taken.

Travel History:

Recent travel to certain geographic regions or countries may temporarily defer individual from donating blood. This is because travelling to specific areas can potentially expose individuals to infectious diseases which can be transmitted through blood transfusion and may pose a risk to the safety of the donated blood.
During the screening process it is essential to inform the medical team about any recent travel history, this is done to ensure the safety of the donated blood and minimize the risk of transmitting infectious blood to recipients.

Pregnancy and
Breastfeeding:

Due to potential risks to both the donor and the baby, pregnant or breastfeeding individuals are temporarily deferred from donating blood. This deferral period allows for the preservation of the health of both the individual and the baby.
